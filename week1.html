Question of the week - What makes a front-end developer

Personally, i've always seen the world of front-end development divided into two sections; front-end designers and front-end developers. The designers mostly focusing on HTML and CSS, creating amazing layouts and designs for websites. The developers diving more into frameworks and JavaScript, working with logic and data. Using this logic I personally call myself more of a front-end developer over a designer, my skills lay mostly in JavaScript.

The thing that both front-end developers and front-end designers have in common in my opinion is that they are the link between designers and back-end developers. Taking a concept made by a designer and preparing it for the back-end developer to make it work. This is why a team can not exist without a front-ender, whether designer or developer. There needs to be a link made between a static design and a working project since neither would be complete without the other.
